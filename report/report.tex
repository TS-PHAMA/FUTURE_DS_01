\subsection{ANOVA — Significance Testing}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{@{}p{5cm} p{3cm} p{3cm}@{}}
\toprule
\rowcolor{primary!10}
\textbf{Test} & \textbf{p-value} & \textbf{Result} \\
\midrule
Profit $\sim$ Category & $3.47 \times 10^{-24}$ & \textcolor{profitgreen}{\textbf{Highly Significant}} \\
Profit $\sim$ Region & 0.049 & \textcolor{profitgreen}{\textbf{Significant}} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{ANOVA Results}
\end{table}

\textbf{Tukey HSD Findings:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Technology outperforms Furniture} by approximately \$70 per order (p < 0.001)
    \item \textbf{Technology outperforms Office Supplies} by approximately \$58 per order (p < 0.001)
    \item \textbf{West outperforms Central} by approximately \$17 per order (p = 0.043)
    \item Other regional differences are not statistically significant
\end{itemize}

\section{Recommendations}

\subsection{Immediate Actions (0--3 Months)}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\color{warningred}\textbf{\theenumi.}, leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Cap discounts at 20\%} — 14\% of orders with high discounts are destroying value
    \item \textbf{Investigate Furniture pricing} — 2.49\% margin is unsustainable; review Tables and Bookcases
    \item \textbf{Audit Texas, Ohio, Pennsylvania} — These states show high sales but significant losses
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Strategic Initiatives (3--12 Months)}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\color{accent}\textbf{\theenumi.}, leftmargin=*]
    \setcounter{enumi}{3}
    \item \textbf{Replicate West region best practices} — Document and deploy the 14.9\% margin model
    \item \textbf{Prioritize Technology inventory} — Generates \$70 more profit per order than Furniture
    \item \textbf{Restructure South region} — Sales-profit correlation near zero requires fundamental changes
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Risk Management}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\color{gold}\textbf{\theenumi.}, leftmargin=*]
    \setcounter{enumi}{6}
    \item \textbf{Implement discount approval workflow} — Require management sign-off for discounts above 20\%
    \item \textbf{Exit or restructure loss-making sub-categories} — Discontinue consistently unprofitable lines
    \item \textbf{Set regional profit margin targets} — Address Central region's 7.92\% margin gap
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Estimated Financial Impact}

\begin{tcolorbox}[
    colback=profitgreen!5,
    colframe=profitgreen!50,
    arc=8pt,
    left=15pt,
    right=15pt
]
Implementing these recommendations could improve overall profit margin by \textbf{2--4 percentage points}, representing approximately \textbf{\$46,000--\$92,000 in additional annual profit}.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Conclusion}

\begin{tcolorbox}[
    colback=primary!5,
    colframe=primary!50,
    arc=8pt,
    left=15pt,
    right=15pt,
    top=15pt,

The business demonstrates strong overall performance with \textbf{\$2.3M in revenue} and \textbf{12.47\% profit margin}. However, significant value is being destroyed in specific areas that require immediate management attention.

\vspace{8pt}

\textbf{Key Takeaways:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Technology and Office Supplies} are the profit engines, each with margins above 17\%
    \item \textbf{West region} represents the operational excellence model with 14.9\% margins
    \item \textbf{Furniture category} (2.49\% margin) and \textbf{Central region} (7.92\% margin) require strategic intervention
    \item \textbf{Excessive discounting} on 14\% of orders systematically erodes profitability
    \item Statistical evidence confirms that category and regional differences are \textbf{statistically significant}
\end{itemize}

\vspace{8pt}

The data clearly indicates that targeted interventions — rather than broad strategic changes — will yield the greatest return on management effort. By focusing on the specific problem areas identified in this analysis, the business can protect and grow profitability while maintaining revenue momentum.
